Teaching formula

Where / Who / When / Why

BibleMapped reads Scripture in context. Each Old Testament section becomes clearer when you ask four steady questions.

Where

Place

Locate the event: Eden, Canaan, Egypt, Sinai, Jerusalem, Babylon, or the return route home.

Who

People

Follow Adam, Noah, Abraham, Israel, Moses, David, the prophets, and the returned community.

When

Timeline

See how creation, covenant, kingdom, judgment, exile, and restoration unfold in sequence.

Why

Meaning

Ask what God is revealing about covenant, holiness, mercy, justice, worship, and hope.

Connected story

Seven movements of the Old Testament

Choose a movement to see its place in the whole story.

Genesis 1-11

Creation

God creates a good world, humanity rebels, violence spreads, and the nations scatter. The need for blessing is global from the beginning.

Where
Eden, the flood world, Babel
Why
The story begins with creation, sin, judgment, mercy, and promise.

Genesis 12-50

Patriarchs

God calls Abraham and forms a covenant family through Sarah, Isaac, Rebekah, Jacob, Leah, Rachel, Joseph, and the tribes of Israel.

Where
Ur, Haran, Canaan, Egypt
Why
God promises land, descendants, and blessing for the nations.

Exodus-Deuteronomy

Exodus

Israel is delivered from Egypt, brought through the sea, given the law at Sinai, and formed as a covenant people in the wilderness.

Where
Egypt, Red Sea, Sinai, wilderness
Why
Rescue leads to worship, holiness, and life with God.

Joshua-1 Kings 11

Kingdom

Israel enters the land, cycles through judges, and becomes a united kingdom under Saul, David, and Solomon with Jerusalem at the center.

Where
Canaan, Jerusalem, the temple
Why
The kingdom shows both the promise and danger of power under covenant.

1 Kings 12-2 Kings 17

Divided Kingdom

The kingdom splits into Israel in the north and Judah in the south. Prophets call both kingdoms back to justice, worship, and covenant faithfulness.

Where
Samaria, Jerusalem, surrounding empires
Why
Division exposes the cost of idolatry and injustice.

2 Kings 25; Prophets

Exile

Assyria and Babylon overtake Israel and Judah. Jerusalem falls, the temple is destroyed, and the people wrestle with judgment and hope far from home.

Where
Samaria, Jerusalem, Babylon
Why
Exile is judgment, but the prophets keep restoration in view.

Ezra-Nehemiah

Return

God brings a remnant home. The altar, temple, and walls are rebuilt, but the story still waits for deeper renewal and lasting hope.

Where
Persia, Jerusalem, rebuilt Judah
Why
Return proves God keeps covenant mercy alive after judgment.
