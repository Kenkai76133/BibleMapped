About

Bible Mapped helps you understand where Scripture happened.

The Gospel comes first.

None of this matters if Jesus did not rise from the dead. And He did. This guide walks you through the question of salvation with Scripture and clear steps.

Question 1

If you died tonight, would you know for sure that you are going to heaven?

This is not meant to scare you. It is meant to bring the most important question into focus.

Question 2

If God asked why He should let you into heaven, what would your answer be?

Many people answer with good works, religion, sincerity, or trying hard. But the Bible starts with our need for grace.

Step 1

All have sinned.

The Bible says every person has sinned before a holy God. We do not start with self-improvement. We start with truth.

Romans 3:23
Step 2

Sin brings death, but God offers life.

Sin separates us from God, and the wages of sin is death. But the gift of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord.

Romans 6:23
Step 3

Christ died for us.

God showed His love toward us in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us. The center of salvation is what Jesus did.

Romans 5:8
Step 4

Believe and call on the Lord.

The Bible connects salvation to faith in Christ, confession, and calling on the Lord from the heart.

Romans 10:9–10, 13
Prayer

A prayer of confession and faith.

Lord Jesus, I know that I have sinned. I believe You died for my sins and rose again. I turn to You, trust You, and ask You to save me. Thank You for Your grace and forgiveness. Amen.

The prayer is not magic. These words do not save by themselves. What matters is true faith in Jesus Christ — trusting Him from the heart.

Meet Calvin

I am a Christian, a husband, a father, and the heart behind Bible Mapped.

I was saved in 2008, and since then, I have loved telling people about Jesus Christ. Over the years, I’ve walked through faith, doubt, Bible college, church hurt, and questions about God. But through it all, I keep coming back to this conviction:

God’s Word is true, even when man fails.

Bible Mapped was born from that journey.

It exists to help people slow down, look closer, and see the Bible as a real story grounded in real places, real people, real history, language, and meaning — all pointing to Jesus Christ.

How Bible Mapped Works

“The Bible does not float above history. It is inside history.”

Bible Mapped helps people see Scripture through real places, real people, real timelines, and real meaning. From Abraham and Lot to Eden, Jerusalem, and the Kidron Valley, the Bible tells a story grounded in history and aimed at the human condition.

This is not about moving people into a denomination. It is about helping people follow the trail of Scripture and see how the whole story points to Jesus Christ: His life, death, and resurrection.
