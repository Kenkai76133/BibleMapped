About BibleMapped

Why BibleMapped Exists

BibleMapped exists because the Bible did not happen in a vacuum. It happened somewhere.

It happened in gardens, deserts, mountains, rivers, cities, temples, roads, prisons, battlefields, fishing villages, and empty tombs.

It is about helping people slow down, look closer, and see the Bible as a real story grounded in real places, real people, real history, real language, and real meaning.

Bible Mapped was created to help people see that the Bible is not myth, not motivational material, and not a collection of disconnected religious stories.

It is the true Word of the living God.

It happened in real places.
It was given through real history.
It reveals the real Savior.

Q&A belongs here too: direct Bible questions, cultural claims, objections, and common misunderstandings answered with Scripture and context.

Are You Going to Heaven?

If you died tonight, would you know for sure you would go to heaven?

Not based on guessing. Not based on being good enough. The Bible gives a clear answer.

If God asked you, “Why should I let you into heaven?” what would you say?

AnswerI tried to be a good person
AnswerI went to church
AnswerI believe in God
AnswerI trusted in Jesus Christ
AnswerI am not sure
AnswerNone of the above

I believe Jesus Christ is God in the flesh. He is not merely a teacher, a prophet, or a religious example. He is the eternal Son of God who came into the world, lived without sin, died for sinners, rose again, and is coming again.

I believe the Trinity is biblical. There is one God, eternally existing as Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. We see this clearly at the baptism of Jesus: the Son is baptized, the Spirit descends, and the Father speaks from heaven.

I believe baptism is obedience, but it does not save. Salvation is by grace through faith in Jesus Christ. Baptism is a public declaration of faith, not the source of it.

Romans 3:23 Romans 6:23 John 3:16 John 14:6 Ephesians 2:8-9 Romans 10:9-13 1 Corinthians 15:3-4

The question is not, “Are you religious?”

The question is, “Have you trusted Jesus Christ?”

The Bible teaches that all have sinned and come short of the glory of God. Sin separates us from God, and no amount of good works can erase our guilt.

But God loved the world and gave His Son. Jesus Christ died for our sins, was buried, and rose again. Salvation is not earned by being good enough. It is received by grace through faith in Christ.

That is the good news.

The BibleMapped Formula

The BibleMapped Formula

The where matters.

Because when you understand where something happened, you begin to see the story more clearly. A mountain is not just a mountain. A wilderness is not just empty space. A road is not just a path between two cities.

In Scripture, places carry meaning. Geography creates context. Context brings clarity.

The maps matter. The timelines matter. The history matters. The geography, archaeology, culture, and original languages all matter. But they only matter because they help us see the greater story God is telling: the story of redemption through His Son.

Where

Places give Scripture setting, movement, and historical texture.

Who

People reveal conflict, covenant, faith, failure, and witness.

Why

Meaning comes into focus as context points us toward Christ.
