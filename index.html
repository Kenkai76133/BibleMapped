Site architecture

Build the library

This section gives you a framework for books, characters, and study paths. You can duplicate these cards as your site grows.

What each page can include

Instead of just naming a person or book, each page can act like a mini-documentary.

Context: historical background, date range, setting, and surrounding events.
Story: what happened, in order, and why it matters in the broader biblical narrative.
Case study: a focused analysis on a major moment, conflict, theme, or turning point.
Map layer: locations, routes, nations, cities, or regional significance.
Why it matters: theological meaning, practical insight, and connections to Christ.

Abraham

Character profile Promise Journey
Featured lineup

Who he is

Write a simple summary of Abraham’s place in the Bible and why he becomes central to both the Old and New Testaments.

Story behind the person

Trace his movement from Ur to Canaan, the covenant promise, the birth of Isaac, and the testing of faith.

Practical takeaway

Connect his life to trust, obedience, waiting, and the cost of faith when the outcome is still unseen.

Case study angles

  • Leaving Ur and the risk of obedience
  • The covenant promise versus biological reality
  • Mount Moriah and radical trust
  • How Abraham points forward to Christ
