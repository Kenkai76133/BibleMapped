Definition

What happens in Genesis?

Genesis introduces the world, humanity, sin, and God’s promise to bless all nations through one family. From creation to the patriarchs, it sets the stage for the story of redemption.

Chapters & Structure

Primeval History (1–11). Creation, Fall, Flood, Nations.

Abraham (12–25). Call of Abram, covenant, Isaac’s birth.

Isaac & Jacob (26–36). Isaac’s life, Jacob’s journey and family.

Joseph (37–50). Joseph’s dreams, betrayal, and rise in Egypt.

People in Genesis

Adam & Eve – The first humans placed in Eden.

Noah – Builder of the ark, survivor of the flood.

Abraham & Sarah – Parents of Isaac, recipients of the covenant.

Jacob – Renamed Israel; father of the twelve tribes.

Joseph – Betrayed by brothers, becomes ruler in Egypt.

Places in Genesis

Eden – The garden paradise where humanity begins.

Ararat – Mountains where the ark came to rest.

Babel – City where languages were confused.

Canaan – Land promised to Abraham’s descendants.

Egypt – Place of Joseph’s rise and family’s refuge.

Themes & Meaning

Divine Sovereignty – God speaks the world into existence and directs history.

Human Sin & Grace – Rebellion brings judgment; grace offers new beginnings.

Covenant – Promises to Noah and Abraham reveal God’s plan for redemption.

Promise & Blessing – God’s blessing flows through Abraham to all nations.

● Red Flags

Genesis does not specify the fruit in Eden – avoid speculative symbolism.

Avoid flattening ancient narratives into modern categories before understanding context.

Remember that genealogies and numbers serve theological as well as historical purposes.
